Hello there!

My name is Gennevi and I am an undergraduate student at the University of Washington pursuing a dual degree in architecture and construction management with a minor in global health.

Many ask how I can alleviate poverty with a career that strays so far from politics and education. But it is simple, as an architect I have the power to physically rebuild the world. Architects have the ability to create environments that can change the course of a community. We see wasted efforts of many volunteers trying to take people out of impoverished neighborhoods or creating what are initiatives of temporary hope. This method does not work because it not only is unsustainable but inconvenient to both parties. My approach is to bring access and resources to impoverished communities with sustainably built schools, libraries and other social facilities. This method has been proven to be successful from large scale projects such as in Medellin, Colombia with the Poyrcto Urbano Integral (PUI) initiative and small projects such as a community garden in North Carolina.
